Temples of Varanasi

HomeTemples

Vishwanath Temple – One of the 12 Jyotirlingas, Kashi Vishwanath is the holiest Shiva temple, symbolizing liberation and divine energy.

Kaal Bhairav Temple – Dedicated to Kaal Bhairav, the protector of Kashi, where devotees seek blessings for protection and power.

Bharat Mata Mandir – A patriotic temple featuring a detailed marble relief map of India, symbolizing national unity.



Tulsi Manas Temple – Built at the site where Tulsidas wrote Ramcharitmanas , with walls inscribed with its sacred verses.

Vishwanath Temple (BHU) – A grand replica of Kashi Vishwanath inside BHU, promoting knowledge and devotion, open to all faiths.

Tridev Mandir – A unique temple dedicated to Brahma, Vishnu, and Mahesh, along with Sai Baba and other deities.



Vishalakshi Temple – A revered Shakti Peetha where Goddess Vishalakshi blesses devotees with prosperity and well-being.

Durga Temple – A striking red temple of Goddess Durga, known for its divine energy and the presence of monkeys.

Sankat Mochan Temple – A famous Hanuman temple founded by Tulsidas, believed to remove troubles and misfortunes.



Batuk Bhairav Temple – Worshipped in his child form, Lord Batuk Bhairav is associated with tantric rituals and an eternal sacred flame.

ISKCON Varanasi – A peaceful Krishna temple offering devotional chanting, Bhagavad Gita teachings, and a serene spiritual atmosphere.

Mani Mandir – A peaceful temple with deep spiritual significance, ideal for meditation and seeking blessings.
